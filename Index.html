🪱 Gutworm

Prefer moderate temperatures and moist pasture. Eggs hatch in dung, larvae migrate onto grass. Temperature weighted most strongly; moisture is also critical.

🐛 Lungworm

Rely heavily on wet pasture to spread from dung onto grass. Rainfall and soil moisture are weighted higher than temperature. Cool, damp outbreaks are common.

🐌 Liver Fluke

Depend on the mud snail as an intermediate host. Snails need very wet, marshy ground. Soil moisture and rainfall dominate the risk weighting.

🧵 Hair Worm

Eggs are hardy and survive in soil for long periods. Moderate warmth and some moisture are needed for infectivity. Less dependent on heavy rain than flukes.

🦠 Coccidia

Multiply rapidly in warm, moist environments. Temperature and moisture are weighted equally; rainfall contributes indirectly by increasing dampness.

🪳 Castor Bean Tick

Ixodes ricinus — most active in cool, humid conditions in spring and autumn. Found in rough pasture and woodland edges. Depends on humidity rather than direct rainfall.
